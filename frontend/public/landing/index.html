The problem

High-value land is unwatched, and watching it is too expensive.

The Mediterranean is the most wildfire-exposed region in Europe, and the risk is rising every season. Yet a private vineyard or estate can't justify the capex of fixed detection towers or a dense ground-sensor grid — so between rare guard rounds, the land is simply not observed. By the time an uncertain signal becomes a confirmed event, it's late.

How it works · the patrol loop

SwarmOS decides. The operator supervises.

SwarmOS is the autonomous layer that decides, plans and coordinates the fleet. Every number an operator sees comes from SwarmOS or an honest simulator — never invented in the interface.

The wedge

Everyone credible is fixed. SWARM is mobile.

Towers, ground sensors and fixed docking stations all carry capex from tens of thousands of euros up — which locks them to public land, utilities and heavy industry, and leaves private mid-size territory structurally unserved.

Fixed infrastructure

  • Camera towers, ground-sensor grids, fixed docks
  • Capex from tens of thousands per site
  • Tied to one location; detection without response
  • Uneconomic for a single private estate

SWARM · mobile patrol

  • Drones as mobile sensors — no fixed install
  • Capex-free MVP: one drone, an operator, the Console
  • Coverage + verification + evidence + escalation
  • One loop across event classes; the OS is the moat

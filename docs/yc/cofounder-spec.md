# SWARM — cofounder profile & search (gap #4)

> Closes gap #4 in [`readiness-and-gaps.md`](readiness-and-gaps.md). YC weights
> team heavily; a clear, honest cofounder story beats a vague one. You chose
> "have / actively recruiting." This defines *who*, *why*, *where to find them*,
> and *how to frame it* in the application + interview.

## The gap to fill (be honest about your own lanes)

You are strong where it's hardest to hire: autonomy/coordination software,
systems, security — you built SwarmOS largely solo. The complementary gaps,
ranked by what de-risks SWARM most:

1. **Flight / robotics / hardware ops** — someone who has actually flown
   missions, knows PX4/MAVLink on real airframes, payloads, and field failure
   modes. This directly attacks the "has it ever flown" risk.
2. **Domain + commercial (land-risk / buyer access)** — someone close to
   wildfire/forestry/estate operations or agri/territorial security sales, who
   shortens the path to the first paying pilot.

A great cofounder covers **#1 or #2**, not a clone of your software strength.
Don't add a third software founder.

## Ideal profile

- Built or operated real drone/robotics systems **in the field** (not just sim),
  OR has direct buyer access + domain credibility in land-risk/wildfire/agri.
- Comfortable with hardware reality, regulation, and supervised field ops.
- Wants to be **all-in** — a cofounder, not a contractor or an advisor.
- Complementary, low-ego, can disagree with you well. You'll spend years together.

## Where to find them

- **Drone/UAV operator communities** in Italy/EU; PX4/ArduPilot/MAVLink open-
  source contributors; university robotics/aerospace labs (PoliTo, PoliMi).
- **Wildfire/forestry/Protezione Civile** circles and agritech for the domain side.
- **Bocconi / B4i** network for a commercial cofounder once you start.
- YC's own **cofounder matching** platform (startschool.org / YC co-founder match).
- Your discovery interviews ([`customer-discovery-kit.md`](customer-discovery-kit.md))
  are a natural funnel — a sharp drone operator you interview could become the hire.

## How to frame it (application + interview)

- **If you have a committed cofounder:** name them, one line on the
  complementary strength, why you two. Done.
- **If still recruiting:** say so plainly — "I built the technical core solo and
  am actively recruiting a cofounder for [flight-ops / commercial], because
  that's the lane I'm weakest in and the one that most de-risks the company."
  YC respects a founder who knows their gap and is closing it honestly far more
  than a fabricated team. Do **not** elevate an advisor or a friend to
  "cofounder" for optics — they probe this.

## The honest test before you add anyone

Would you give this person 20–40% of the company and trust them to make
hard calls when you're not in the room? If not, they're a hire or an advisor,
not a cofounder. A wrong cofounder is worse than solo — and YC knows it.

03 · Manifesto
— A new category
We wrote the future.
Then we brought it here.
Abbiamo scritto il futuro.
Poi lo abbiamo portato qui.

SWARM is a future-consumer-technology brand built on autonomous, distributed intelligence — the spiritual successor of the Dormio family. Not a startup of drones; a new category where autonomy becomes simple, desirable, and iconic.

Many units. One intention. The voice mirrors the product — coordinated and quiet. SWARM does not justify itself, does not bounce, does not whoosh. It arrives.

Color appears only when something activates. Type whispers. Negative space is a feature. The ring is the signature. The ellipse is the memory.

SWARM publishes in English and Italian with matching weight. Each Italian claim has the rhythm of its English counterpart. Periods are weapons.

— and yet, still SWARM.
The rule. Cover the wordmark with your thumb. If the orbital ellipses, the mist gradient, and the serif rhythm still carry the brand on their own — ship it. If they don't — quiet it down. Recognition lives in the geometry, not in the letters.

The signature. Every SWARM artefact carries the same three threads: a ring (origin), an ellipse (orbit), and a wide-tracked serif (the wordmark). Together they form a single trace that reads in two seconds, in any size, in any tongue.

07 · The ellipse
The universal signature
— Geometry
Where the ring goes, the orbit follows.
— Variants
01 · flat
Horizon. From above.
02 · shallow
Distant orbit.
03 · canonical
The default.
04 · circle
Head-on. The mark.
Use Always at least one per surface. Stroke at 1.25 px platinum, 1 px gunmetal, or 0.2 mm ink at 18–55 % opacity — never filled. Stack two or three for density. Always perspective-aligned (lower in the canvas implies more tilt). Pair with a single ring above it when the wordmark is present.
Avoid No closed ellipses with fill. No gradient strokes. No more than four stacked — the rhythm collapses past four. Never rotated off the horizontal axis (max ±2°). Never crossed by another ellipse. Never broken by the wordmark — let the wordmark sit through, not over.

Rule 01

Black is the canvas.

A SWARM page is more black than content. Never a white default. Never a gradient on chrome — only the mist on brand surfaces.

Rule 02

Accents are state.

Blue, green, amber are not decoration. If it isn't a status, it isn't allowed to use them.

Rule 03

Borders, not shadows.

1 px Gunmetal hairlines + 1 px inset platinum highlight on the top edge. Machined, never soft.

Three colors. Each one a state, never a decoration.

Orbital Blue

#B8FF66
Online · confirmed · in flight · the system is doing what it said it would.
Attention
Launch Amber
#FFB45C
Pre-launch · low warning · a unit asks to be looked at, not feared.
Activation accents — state only.
A blue on a marketing page that doesn't represent a state is wrong. A green is operational. An amber is attention. Nothing decorative. Nothing aesthetic. The accent is a verb, not an adjective.

10 · The 85 % rule
When color is allowed
Eighty-five per cent of every spread is black, platinum, or silver. The rest is permission.
— Visual budget
85 % Monochrome
5 %Connected
5 %Operational
5 %Attention
Allowed
When color fires.
  • · The ring lights — a unit comes online.
  • · A pill announces status — 007 / 128 online.
  • · A focus ring forms — the orbital blue, by definition.
  • · A map node pulses once — attention requested.
Forbidden
When color is wrong.
  • · Headlines in blue. The brand does not paint.
  • · Gradient backgrounds on UI panels.
  • · Hover states that swap color. Brightness only.
  • · Two activation accents in the same component.

Rules
  • No drop-shadows. Borders only. The hairline is the elevation.
